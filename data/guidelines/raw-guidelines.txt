SECTION 1
About This Guide
WaiverPro is a web-based platform for managing healthcare regulatory waiver requests from
submission through approval. This manual explains how to navigate the application, submit and
track waiver requests, manage facilities and users, and obtain support.
Who should use this guide
This guide is written for provider-portal users — facility administrators and staff who prepare,
submit, and monitor waiver applications on behalf of their organisation.
Scope
This document describes the provider-facing web application. Every screen, label, and button
described here reflects the live product. Figures show live account data; the specific records on your
screen will differ from the examples pictured.
CONVENTIONS
Button labels and on-screen text are shown in bold. Web addresses and field values are shown in
monospace. Each section also lists the URL path of the screen it describes, relative to the site’s base
address.

SECTION 2
Accessing WaiverPro — The Landing Page
URL /
Open WaiverPro in your web browser. The public landing page introduces the platform and lists the
waiver programs currently available.
Figure 2.1 — The WaiverPro landing page: heading, search bar, and the Getting Started button.
Key elements of the landing page:
Heading — “Healthcare Waiver Management”, with the tagline “Streamline your regulatory
waiver process from submission to approval”.
Search waivers — the search box (placeholder Search waivers) filters the program list by
name.
All Waiver Programs — quick-filter buttons name each program, and each published program
appears as a card showing its name, description, Processing Time, Waiver Period, and an

“Expired”, with the dates it was available).
Getting Started — the button in the top-right corner takes you to the sign-in screen.
Footer — links to Privacy Policy and Terms of Service, with the line “Copyright © 2025
WaiverPro”.

Figure 2.2 — The All Waiver Programs section, listing each program as a detailed card.
To begin, click Getting Started in the top-right corner of the page.

SECTION 3
Signing In
URL /login
The sign-in screen authenticates you into the provider portal.
Figure 3.1 — The sign-in screen.
1. Enter your Email (placeholder m@example.com).
2. Enter your Password. Passwords must be at least 6 characters.
3. Click Login.
If you have forgotten your password, use the Forgot your password? link beside the password
field to begin recovery.
After a successful sign-in you are taken to the My Applications dashboard.

SECTION 4
The Application Workspace & Navigation
URL Shared by all signed-in pages (/dashboard/...)
Once signed in, every page shares a common layout: a fixed sidebar on the left and a header along
the top.
Figure 4.1 — The signed-in workspace. The sidebar (left) provides navigation; the header (top) provides search,
notifications, and your account menu.
The sidebar
At the top of the sidebar the brand WaiverPro appears with the label “Healthcare Waiver
Management”, followed by the primary action button + New Application. The navigation items, in
order, are:
My Applications — your waiver applications dashboard.
Facilities — facilities you manage or belong to.

Action Items — pending items that require your attention.
User Management — manage the users in your organisation.
Announcements — system and program updates.
Settings — account and preference settings.
Below a Support heading, four further items appear: FAQs, Tickets, Contact, and Take a Tour
(which launches a guided walkthrough of the interface).
The header
Search — a search box for the current page.
Notifications — a bell icon with a badge showing the number of outstanding action items.
Account — your initials appear as an avatar; opening it reveals your email address and a
Logout button.
TIP
The + New Application button is available from the sidebar on every page, so you can start a waiver
request at any time.

SECTION 5
My Applications
URL /dashboard/my-applications
The My Applications dashboard is your home screen. It lists your waiver applications and
summarises them in two charts.
Figure 5.1 — The My Applications dashboard: waiver-type tabs, status filters, the applications table, and the two
summary charts.
Filtering the list
Two layers of filters narrow the table, each showing a live count:
Waiver-type tabs — All, Patient Needs Waiver (PNW) Request, Program Flexibility
(PF) Request, and Workforce Shortage Waiver (WSW) Request.
Status chips — All Applications, Draft, Submitted, Reopen, In Review, Rejected,
Approved, Evaluator Review, Manager Review, Revoked, and Expired.

The applications table
Applications are listed with the columns Waiver ID, Name, Facility, Type, Status, and Created
on. Use the Rows per page control and the page arrows to page through results.
Summary charts
Applications by Type — a doughnut chart breaking your applications down by waiver type
(PNW, PF, WSW).
Applications Overview — a doughnut chart breaking your applications down by status.

SECTION 6
Submitting a New Waiver Application
URL Opens as a panel over the current page (no dedicated URL)
Click + New Application in the sidebar to open the application panel. A progress bar and the step
tabs Waiver Type, Select Location, and Fill Application show where you are.
Figure 6.1 — The New Waiver Application panel, open at step one, “Select Waiver Type”.
Step 1 — Waiver Type
Under “Select Waiver Type”, choose the waiver request that best matches your needs. Each type —
Patient Needs Waiver (PNW) Request, Program Flexibility (PF) Request, and
Workforce Shortage Waiver (WSW) Request — appears as a selectable card with a
description. A type that is not currently open is marked (for example, “Expired”) and cannot be
selected; the panel notes the dates it was available.
Step 2 — Select Location
Choose the facility (and facility type) the request applies to.
Step 3 — Fill Application
Complete the request details — contact number, dates, description, supporting documents, and the
required acknowledgement — then submit.

Use Next to advance through the steps; you can return to an earlier step to make changes, and
submit your completed request on the final step. The × button in the top-right corner closes the
panel at any time.

SECTION 7
Facilities
URL /dashboard/facilities
The Facilities page lists the facilities in your organisation and tracks the status of their waiver
applications.
Figure 7.1 — The Facilities page, with status filters, the facilities table, and the Facilities Status Overview.
Filters
A row of status chips filters by application due-date status: All Facilities, Due Soon, Upcoming,
Scheduled, Overdue, and No Status. You can further filter by Role and Type, or search by
name.

NOTE
The status chips and the Status / License Exp Date columns are part of the Facilities Status
Tracking feature, which can be turned on or off under Settings → Features.
The facilities table
Facilities are listed with the columns Name, Type, License Number, License Exp Date, and
Status. Below the table, the Facilities Status Overview summarises the portfolio. Use the paging
controls to move through results.

SECTION 8
Action Items
URL /dashboard/action-items
The Action Items page collects pending items that require your attention.
Figure 8.1 — The Action Items page, with the items table and the status summary chart.
Items are listed with the columns ID, Name, Status, Priority, and Due Date. Each Name links
to the relevant record so you can act on it. You can filter the list by Status and Priority using the
controls at the top right.
Status values include Done and In Process.
Priority values are High, Medium, and Low.
Due Date shows how far an item is overdue or when it is due.

A summary doughnut chart shows the total number of action items broken down by priority. The
same outstanding count is reflected by the notification badge in the header.

SECTION 9
User Management
URL /dashboard/user-management
The User Management page lets organisation administrators view and manage the people who
can access WaiverPro on behalf of the organisation.
Figure 9.1 — The User Management page.
Two tabs separate current Members from Pending Invites, each showing a count. The table lists
each user with the columns Name, Email, Associated Facilities, Last Updated, and Actions
(edit and remove). To add someone, click Invite User in the top-right corner. Use the search box to
locate a specific user, and the paging controls to move through the list.

SECTION 10
Announcements
URL /dashboard/announcements
The Announcements page keeps you informed: “Stay up to date with important system and
program updates.”
Figure 10.1 — The Announcements page.
Each announcement shows its title, a status badge — Active, Upcoming, or Expired — an
optional priority badge (High, Medium, or Low), and its date. Click an announcement to expand
and read its full detail. A search box helps you find a specific announcement.

SECTION 11
Support — FAQs, Tickets & Contact
The Support section of the sidebar offers three ways to get help.
FAQs
URL /dashboard/faqs
The FAQs page answers common questions about WaiverPro. Click any question to expand its
answer.
Figure 11.1 — The FAQs page.
Support Tickets
URL /dashboard/tickets

The Tickets page (“Support Tickets — Track and manage your support tickets.”) lists your tickets as
cards showing the ticket number, status and priority badges, and the created/updated times. Filter
by Status and Priority, and open any ticket with View Details. Click + New Ticket to raise one.
Figure 11.2 — The Support Tickets page.
In the Create New Support Ticket panel, provide a Title, choose the Related Application,
select a Category and Sub Category, add a Description, attach any files, and click Create
Ticket.

Figure 11.3 — The Create New Support Ticket panel.
Contact
URL /dashboard/contact
The Contact page (“Get in touch with our support team”) provides a message form and the support
team’s direct details.

SECTION 12
Settings
URL /dashboard/settings
The Settings page (“Manage your account and preferences”) shows your organisation and is
organised into four cards.
Figure 12.1 — The Settings page.
Organisation — the organisation your account belongs to is shown at the top of the page.
Profile Information — your first name, last name, email (read-only), and phone number. Click
Save Changes to update.
Notifications — toggles for Email Notifications, Status Updates, and Action Items.

Security — change your password. A new password must be at least 6 characters long. Click
Change Password to apply.
Features — toggle experimental or upcoming features: Facilities Status Tracking (shows
application due dates and status on the Facilities page) and the FAQ Assistant Chatbot (off by
default).
